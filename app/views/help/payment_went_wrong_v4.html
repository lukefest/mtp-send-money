Help Common issues with payments

Occasionally, something can go wrong with a payment. These are the most common issues:

Payment did not reach the prisoner

On rare occasions a payment can take longer than 3 days. In most cases the money has arrived but not yet gone into the prisoner’s spend allowance. Wait a few more days then if you’re still concerned contact the prison.

I made a bank transfer from a bank branch

What to do

If you made a bank transfer in person at a high street bank branch your identitiy was not recorded. This means the payment counts as anonymous and we can’t credit the prisoner. You’ll need to request a refund.

I made a bank transfer from a non UK bank account

What to do

If you made a bank transfer from an overseas bank account, we won't know your identitiy. This means the payment counts as anonymous and we can’t credit the prisoner. You’ll need to request a refund.

Please note, this does not apply to debit card payments which can be made from overseas, as long as they are made by Visa or Mastercard.

Bank transfer was refunded unexpectedly

You may have used an incorrect prisoner reference in your bank transfer so we cannot identify the prisoner. The refund will be from ‘GBS RE NOMS AGENCY’ in your bank statement. The prisoner reference used must be exactly as written.

Confirmation email was not received

If you paid by debit card, please check your spam/junk folder. Otherwise, you may have mis-typed your email address but the payment will still have gone through.
